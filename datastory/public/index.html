The Lure of Unfinished Stories

Movies are a universal form of entertainment that appeal to audiences worldwide. But have you ever been let down by a great movie that abruptly ends with a cliffhanger? Sometimes, there is no sequel and you need to live with it. Sometimes, when there is one, it is simply disappointing...
So, what makes a movie even have a sequel? How about a great one?
In this project, we investigate these questions using a large dataset of movies released between 1980 and 2016. We aim to discover the hidden factors that influence the success of movie sequels, and compare them with movies that do not have sequels.

Part 1 : Comparing movies with sequels to movies without sequels

To start our analysis, we first identified which movies in our dataset had sequels and which did not. We used a combination of web scraping, data cleaning, and manual verification to enrich our dataset with sequel information. We then divided the movies into two groups: movies with sequels and movies without sequels. We compared these groups on various dimensions, with the goal to find out how these two categories differ, and what factors are associated with the success of movie sequels.

Genre Dynamics

Moving on to the intrinsic characteristics of movies, we analyzed the genre composition of movies with and without sequels. Our dataset had a large number of subgenres, which made it difficult to compare movies across different genres. Therefore, we simplified our genre analysis by selecting the top 10 most frequent genres in our dataset, and assigning each movie two main genres based on its subgenres. The heatmap allows us to easily visualize the frequency of genre combinations, and we found that movies with sequels are more likely to belong to the action, adventure, comedy, and fantasy genres, while movies without sequels are more likely to belong to the drama, romance, thriller, and horror genres. This suggests that movies with high entertainment value and spectacle are more prone to getting sequels, whereas original films are more suitable for genres that have high emotional appeal and realism. We also performed a statistical test, which confirms that the genre distribution of movies with and without sequels is significantly different.
