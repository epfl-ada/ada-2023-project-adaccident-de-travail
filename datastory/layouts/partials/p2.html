Part 2 : Comparison between original films and their direct sequels

To dive deeper into the distinctions between original films and sequels, we meticulously crafted datasets pairing each original film with its corresponding sequel. This approach ensured a comprehensive comparison. Special attention was paid to address potential bias caused by film sagas, treating each pair of films independently.

Release Trends Analysis

The temporal analysis of release years highlights that while most sequels followed the trend of immediate releases, others strategically opted for a delayed launch, capitalizing on nostalgia or evolving audience preferences.

Financial Performance

Our approach centers on plotting the evolution of earnings from original movies to their sequels. This visual analysis aims to explore the trends and differences in revenue growth or decline between the original films and their subsequent sequels. The analysis uncovers intriguing patterns. Surprisingly, some sequels surpassed their original films, challenging the conventional belief that the first installment often outperforms its successors, and suggesting that certain sequels manage to captivate audiences and generate significant revenue. The scatter plot reveals a positive correlation between the earnings of original movies and their sequels, indicating that sequels often emulate the financial performance of their predecessors.

Runtime Divergence

Examining mean durations provided insights into how the temporal structure of a film relates to its success. Notably, most sequels exhibited a trend towards identical or extended durations, potentially indicating a more elaborate storyline or a desire to offer audiences a more immersive experience.
